  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 解决复杂问题时，了解干预措施为何有效比仅仅知道是否有效更重要。研究旨在探究因果机制信息是否帮助人们区分不同干预措施，特别是能否让他们认识到处理根本原因的间接干预优于仅处理症状的直接干预。

Method: 采用实验性蹄修剪场景，参与者评估各种干预措施。他们收到包含不同类型因果信息和不同机制详细程度的因果图。研究比较了详细机制信息及其在其他影响因素背景下的呈现对参与者评估的影响。

Result: 详细机制信息及其在影响因素背景下的呈现使参与者对间接干预的怀疑略有减少，但效果很小。更重要的是，这些信息未能改变参与者对仅处理问题症状的干预措施的强烈偏好。

Abstract: When deciding how to solve complex problems, it seems important not only to know whether an intervention is helpful but also to understand why. Therefore, the present study investigated whether explicit information about causal mechanisms enables people to distinguish between multiple interventions. It was hypothesised that mechanism information helps them appreciate indirect interventions that treat the root causes of a problem instead of just fixing its symptoms. This was investigated in an experimental hoof trimming scenario in which participants evaluated various interventions. To do so, they received causal diagrams with different types of causal information and levels of mechanistic detail. While detailed mechanism information and its embedding in the context of other influences made participants less sceptical towards indirect interventions, the effects were quite small.
